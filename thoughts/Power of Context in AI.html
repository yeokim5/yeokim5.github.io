Power of Context in AI

Sep 2025

For a while, people focused on crafting the perfect question, the "prompt" to get the best possible answer from AI. But now the paradigm is shifting. It’s Time to Design a ‘World,’ Not Just a ‘Magic Spell’. We're moving away from finding a single, perfect question and toward designing the entire "environment" and "context" that allows AI to perform at its best. Welcome to the era of Context Engineering.

1. The Dazzling Rise and Shadowy Limits of 'Prompt Engineering'

Since the arrival of Google's Transformer architecture in 2017, the age of Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT has dawned. Users quickly discovered that the quality of an AI's output depended heavily on the command, or "prompt," it was given. "Prompt engineering" became the essential skill, a kind of magic art for coaxing the best answers from the machine.

However, these "magic spells" had two significant limitations when it came to building reliable services:

  • Brittleness: A tiny change a single word or preposition, could drastically alter the outcome. A perfectly crafted prompt could become useless overnight after a minor update to the AI model.
  • Knowledge Cutoff and Hallucination: AI is trained on data only up to a certain point in time. It knows nothing about recent events or private information, like your company's internal documents. This leads to "hallucinations," where the AI confidently fabricates information that sounds plausible but is completely false.

2. The Paradigm Shift: The Dawn of Context Engineering

To overcome these limitations, developers began to change their approach. They moved away from crafting the 'one perfect question' and started 'systematically designing the entire environment an AI needs to produce the best results.' This is the essence of Context Engineering.

What is Context Engineering?

It refers to all activities involved in designing a system that dynamically provides an LLM with the necessary information, tools, and instructions all bundled together as "context" to reliably solve a given task.

Here’s a simple analogy:

  • Past (Prompt Engineering): "Answer this question smartly."
  • Present (Context Engineering): "Here is the relevant PDF manual, a real-time stock price API, and our entire conversation history. Using all of this as context, complete the task."

A key technology that makes context engineering possible is Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). When a user asks a question, the system first retrieves the most relevant pieces of information from a trusted database (like internal company documents or recent news articles). It then feeds both this 'verified factual data' and the original question to the AI. The result is a far more accurate and trustworthy answer.

3. The Future: Autonomous Agentic AI

The ultimate goal of context engineering goes beyond just getting better answers. It's about enabling Agentic AI. AI that can think and act for itself.

An Agentic AI is an autonomous system that can set goals, create plans, use tools (like APIs), and self-correct based on feedback. (Good examples include Cognition Labs' Devin and agent modes in development at OpenAI.)

For such an agent to function properly, it must have a real-time awareness of its entire situation: its mission, its plans, the tools available to it, and its past actions. This comprehensive set of information is its 'context' in essence, it becomes the agent's 'World Model.'

Time To Try Yourself:  Get Deeper Insights by Giving AI Your Personal Context

Now that you understand the importance of context, it's time to experience its power for yourself. You don't need complex RAG technology. Simply providing your own personality as 'context' is enough to see a dramatic difference.

We'll use the globally respected Big Five personality theory.

  1. Go to the Big Five Personality Test website(https://bigfive-test.com/)  and complete the test.
  2. Copy the entire text from your results page.
  3. Go to Google Gemini and paste the text you copied. (At the time of writing, Gemini 2.5 Pro is the best model to try for this experiment)
  4. After pasting your results, enter the first prompt below:

[Paste your full Big Five results here]

Based on these personality results, analyze me as a person. What kind of person am I?

  1. Once the AI provides its analysis of your personality, follow up with a more specific, personal question.

Based on your analysis, what are my strengths and weaknesses? Considering them, what kind of career would suit me? I'm currently interested in [your career of interest]. Is it okay to continue on this path?

Also, for daily advice on relationships (friends, partners), what kind of people am I compatible with, and what attitude should I adopt?

You will likely receive an answer that is worlds apart from what you'd get with a regular LLM question. The response will be far more specific and insightful, almost like talking to a psychological counselor who knows you well.

Interacting with AI is no longer a guessing game. The richness and accuracy of the 'world' (the context) we provide determines its power. Depending on it, AI can become a partner beyond our imagination.
