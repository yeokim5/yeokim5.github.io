Labor Equation

I want to go back when I first got introduced with artificial intelligence, which happened in South Korea back in 2016. As a Korean living there at the time, I cleary remember this remarkable event that captivated the entire nation. It definitely wasn’t a global attention level event, but it definitely dominated the news headlines in Korea, as known as with the name of "Lee Sedol vs AlphaGo".

Fast forward nearly a decade, the progress of AI since then has been nothing but exponential progress with unprecedented growth. Its capabilities seem to double every quarter. We now live in an era where saying "Whenever I need advice, I first go to AI" isn’t a crazy statement and a reasonable one. Imagining uttering these words in 2016, people would have thought you were insane.

All the people who experienced and witnessed the internet and mobile revolutions have been saying the exact same word by word, AI is the biggest revolution they’ve seen in their entire lifetime, which is a really big statement considering how big internet and mobile changed our life. Wheter it’s new products or groundbreaking techniques, AI innovation has not been ceased, which makes a perfect sense when you consider that every major tech companies and every tech billionaires are betting on AI, pouring their immense time and resources.

At this point, everyone, including myself, recognizes AI's immense potential. It's a field where it has grown so much so quickly, a regular AI enthusiast today might be more knowledgeable than a PhD student just five years ago. This acceleration fueled by money + energy + talent + national interest makes it foolish to bet on AI stopping here. It seems inevitable the future of labor will look very different than now. And for the better understaindings of the concept of future labor, I came up with a labor equation to represent how labor are working today, and will likely work on our future.

Phase 1: The Current Labor Paradigm

Currently, we can define Output as a direct product of an individual's Capability and their Effort.

Output = Capability × Effort

  • Output (O): The goal achieved or the completion of a task.
  • Capability (C): An individual's intelligence, skills, and knowledge.
  • Effort (E): The time, energy, and resources applied to a task.

This equation can be simplified as O = C X E . And can be interpreted as to achieve a greater output, one must either enhance their capability or increase their effort. Conversely, it can also imply as that the higher capability of individual allows the completion of larger tasks with less effort E = O / C .

Phase2: AI-Augmented Labor: The Emerging Reality

We are already witnessing this dawn of AI-augmented labor. This isn't a future concept, AI is increasing their capability each day, it’s not hard to find numerous domains that AI is actively shifting the traditional labor. In this new phase, AI aguments and multiplies human output.

Output = ( Human Capability × AI Capability ) × Effort

This upcoming era, individuals who proficiently integrate these tools into their workflows will achieve disproportionately higher results than others who don’t. Imagine a person’s capability is 5, and an AI's capability is 3. The combined output of this person will be an amplified 15. This suggests that single most important factor to achive high output in this era will be determined by how well you can effectively leverage the AI tools.

Phase3: AI-Replaced Labor: The Inevitable Horizon

As AI continues its exponential growth, we will eventually reach a point where it takes over an increasingly larger portion of the "capability" aspect of labor. This will lead to the ultimate labor equation:

Output = AI Capability × Effort

In this phase, the direct value of human capability in many tasks, while perhaps not entirely zero, it will significantly diminish. Human input might become optional for many tasks, as AI driven labor becomes abundant and incredibly efficient.

Other than domains that require significant human values, such as profound creativity, emotional intelligence, complex ethical judgment, and uniquely human understanding. Most of labor will follow this equation.

Such task that requires a combined labor of ten engineers right now might be accomplished with ease by someone with minimal technical expertise, perhaps even a child in future. When "work" in its traditional sense becomes remarkably easy and abundant, our focus will shift from how to get things done to what we are doing. The primary value will lie not in the execution, but in the definition and direction of the output itself.

Navigating the Future: Finding Meaning in Our Actions

Predicting the future, especially with a rapidly evolving technology like AI, has proven challenging. No one fully anticipated the current AI race and its profound implications. However, the trajectory suggests that the rapid automation of labor is not a question of "if" but "when."

Given this inevitable shift, our focus must move beyond simply "getting things done." We must deliberately choose what output we are aiming for.

This means:

  • Focusing on meaning: Seek out projects, companies, or endeavors that resonate with your values and excite your heart.
  • Defining purpose: Instead of blindly solving problems, understand why you are solving them and what ultimate impact you wish to create.
  • Cultivating uniquely human skills: Invest in capabilities that AI cannot easily replicate, such as critical thinking, creativity, complex problem-solving, and emotional intelligence.

The future of labor is not about becoming a cog in an evermore efficient machine. It's about consciously shaping the output of that machine, ensuring our efforts contribute to something meaningful and purposeful.
